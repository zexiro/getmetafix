# How Freelancers Get Paid: 7 Payment Methods Ranked by Security

**The Problem:** You finish the project. Send the deliverable. Then… silence. The client has your work but you don't have your money.

This happens to freelancers every single day. According to Freelancers Union, 71% of freelancers have struggled with non-payment at some point. That's not a bug — it's a feature of how freelance payments work.

Here's the issue: **most payment methods require you to deliver first, then hope you get paid.**

Let's rank the 7 most common freelance payment methods by how well they protect you from getting burned.

## The Rankings (Worst to Best)

### 7. Cash/Check (Security: 2/10)
**How it works:** Client pays with physical cash or mails a check after delivery.

**The problem:** Zero recourse if the check bounces or never arrives. You've already sent the files. By the time you realize they're not paying, they've ghosted.

**Real risk:** High. Checks can bounce weeks later. Cash can be counterfeit. No paper trail for legal action.

**Use case:** Only for clients you've worked with multiple times and trust completely.

---

### 6. Venmo/Cash App (Security: 3/10)
**How it works:** Client sends payment via peer-to-peer app after you deliver.

**The problem:** No purchase protection. No dispute resolution. If they don't pay, you have zero recourse. These apps are designed for friends splitting dinner, not business transactions.

**Real risk:** Moderate to high. People treat these like social payments, not professional ones. Easy to ghost and face no consequences.

**Use case:** Small projects under $100 with low-risk clients.

---

### 5. Wire Transfer (Security: 4/10)
**How it works:** Client sends bank wire after project completion.

**The problem:** International wires can take 3-5 business days and cost $25-50 in fees. Clients can cancel wires before they clear. You're still delivering before payment is guaranteed.

**Real risk:** Moderate. Wire transfers are harder to fake than checks, but reversals do happen, especially with new clients.

**Use case:** Large projects ($5K+) where both parties expect formal payment.

---

### 4. PayPal Invoice (Security: 5/10)
**How it works:** You send an invoice, client pays, then you deliver.

**The problem:** PayPal heavily favors buyers. Clients can open disputes up to 180 days later and often win by claiming "service not rendered" even when you delivered everything. PayPal can freeze your account and hold funds for months.

**Real risk:** Moderate. Better than nothing, but not actual protection. Clients know they can dispute and likely win.

**Use case:** Mid-sized projects ($500-$2K) where you need *some* buyer confidence but want faster payment than wire.

---

### 3. Upwork/Fiverr Escrow (Security: 7/10)
**How it works:** Client deposits money into platform escrow before you start. You deliver, client approves, platform releases funds.

**The problem:** Platform takes 10-20% of your earnings. Clients can still dispute and platform often sides with them. You're locked into their ecosystem and fee structure.

**Real risk:** Low to moderate. You're more likely to get paid, but you're paying a steep premium for that security (20% on Upwork for new freelancers).

**Use case:** Finding new clients when you need the platform's trust infrastructure.

---

### 2. Stripe/Square Invoice with 50% Upfront (Security: 8/10)
**How it works:** Client pays 50% upfront via Stripe/Square invoice. You deliver. They pay remaining 50% before receiving final files.

**The problem:** You're still exposed on the second 50%. If they ghost after the first payment, you've done work you won't get paid for. You need to negotiate this structure for every client.

**Real risk:** Low. Getting 50% upfront filters out non-serious clients. Most clients who pay upfront will pay the remainder.

**Use case:** Larger projects ($2K+) where clients expect milestone payments.

---

### 1. Pay-to-Unlock Delivery (Security: 10/10)
**How it works:** You complete the work. Upload files to a locked delivery link. Client pays the full amount, then automatically receives the files. No manual intervention needed.

**The problem:** This barely exists yet. Most freelancers don't have access to this kind of system.

**Real risk:** Zero. Client can't access files without paying. Payment is guaranteed before delivery. No disputes, no chargebacks, no ghosting.

**Use case:** Every project. Every client. Every time.

## Why Pay-to-Unlock Doesn't Exist Yet

Freelancers have been asking for this for years. "Why can't I just lock the files until they pay?"

The answer: **it requires custom infrastructure** most freelancers can't build themselves. You'd need:
- Secure file hosting
- Payment processing (Stripe, etc.)
- Access control (locked until payment)
- Automated delivery after payment
- Email notifications
- Mobile-friendly client experience

That's a full product, not a quick hack.
